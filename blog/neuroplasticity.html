What is Neuroplasticity?

The brain is constantly rewiring itself and modifying its connection. Neuroplasticity develops our brain allowing us to learn, and form memories. Essentially, its the ability to change and adapt due to experience. Without Neuroplasiticty our brains would not develop from infancy through to adulthood. At birth, every neuron in the cerebral cortex has an estimated 2,500 synapses, or small gaps between neurons where nerve impulses are relayed. By the age of three, this number has grown to around 15,000 synapses per neuron. However, the average adult only has about half that number of synapses. This is because as we gain new experiences, some connections are strengthened while others are eliminated. This process is known as synaptic pruning. Things we hardly use eventually die, alloiwng our brain to learn or experience new things and purge the ones we have no use for. Neuroplasticity also facilitates the process of memory formation and retention. Through repeated practice and reinforcement, connections between neurons become stronger, making it easier to recall information or perform learned tasks. This is important in learning as it allow us to learn new things and the ability to enhance existing cognitive capabilities. Research has shown that sleep is an important role to the growth in the brain. It strengthens the brain and can encourage greater brain plasticity. Understanding neuroplasticity is relevant to learning because it highlights that the brain is not fixed or limited in its capacity to acquire new knowledge. It emphasizes the importance of continuous learning, practice, and exposure to new experiences to promote brain development and enhance cognitive abilities.

Growth Mindset

Growth mindset is a concept developed by psychologist Carol Dweck, which describes the belief that intelligence, abilities, and skills can be developed through dedication, effort, and hard work. It is the belief that one's abilities are not fixed traits but can be improved and expanded over time. It believes tht if people struggle with something, it is not solidified that they can improve over time. There are 5 characteristics of Growth Mindset. Intellignece can be developed, Embrace Challenges, Persist in the face of setbacks, Seeing effort as a path to mastery, and Learn from criticism. All of these characteristics are a positive view-point on learning that we as humans can utilize to further develop whatever we are passionate about.
