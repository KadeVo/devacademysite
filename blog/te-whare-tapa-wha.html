What is Te Whare Tapa Wha?

Te Whare Tapa Wh훮 is a model of the 4 dimensions of wellbeing developed by T훮 Mason Durie in 1984 to provide a M훮ori perspective on health. The 4 dimensions are Taha Tinana, Taha Whanau, Taha Hinengaro and, Taha Wairua. The 4 dimensions have a foundation named Whenua (land).

  • Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing) - This refers to how our body feels and how we take care of it. IT is important to meet your body needs and refuelling it to help feel mentally well.


  • Taha Wh훮nau (Family Wellbeing) - This refers to who makes you feel like you belong, who cares about you and who you share your life with. Whanau is about your extended relationships, those who are in your friend groups, communities and the people you care about have a unique role and contribute to your wellbeing and identity.


  • Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing) - This refers to your mind, heart and feelings which need to be taken care of.


  • Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing) - This focuses on yoru relationship with the environment, people and your heritage in the past, present, and future. This aspect can be viewed differently depending on perspective as it can also contain elements of faith/religion. Essentially wairua is an internal connection to the universe that can widely be perceived.


  • Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment) - Whenua can be thought of as your place of belonging, meaning a space where you feel comfortable and safe to be yourself. It includes our connection to the land as we are linked to elements of the Earth.
