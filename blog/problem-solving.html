Elegantly Problem Solving

During the fizzbuzz exercise where if you input a number, if it is divisible by 15 the computer will say "FizzBuzz", if it is divisible by 5 it will say "Buzz" and if it is divisible by 3 it will say "Fizz". Throughout this exercise I used Pseudocode to begin my code and began console.logging, Googling and reading error messages as I was having numbers divisible by 15 returning "Buzz" which is the indication that it is divisible by 5. I learnt that order matters as it is the process in which the computer will check for conditions. I had 5 the first order so even if the number was divisible by 15, it has already checked off that it is divisible by 5, making it so the computer never checks if it is divisible by 15.

Problem Solving Processes

Below states each problem-solving technique/process and how confident I feel in using them.

  • Pseudocode: I am confident in using it however, I do not feel as if I am the best at writing it out as some concepts still feel foreign to me and I might not write down that makes sense or is applicable to the scenario.


  • Trying something: This method used to be my go to as I would try something new to possibly get a new result or solution but may not fully understand it. It works with simplier problems however, not reliable for complex issues.


  • Rubber ducky method: I am confident in this method as this is something I practice a lot by myself as a general rule. I like to talk out when I do things to make sure it all makes sense to me otherwise I might get lost in the coding process. I also explain my thoughts and how I understand code to DavID which has made me more confident in my understanding of code.


  • Reading error messages: This is something I am not entirely confident in as when I read the error messages, sometimes I do not fully understand the error. However, reading "undefined" errors are more simple than complex errors. Therefore, I find reading error messages useful but not completely as some of the text feels foreign to me.


  • Console.logging: I am getting more confident with console.logging as I recently found out an easy way to utilize the console.log in just a javascript file. Beforehand I would have to have an html page to be able to check.


  • Googling: Googling has been my best tool as I believe every solution is on the internet, however, as things become more complicated I can imagine it being increasingly more difficult to find the help I am seeking on Google.


  • Asking peers ofr help: I have asked help from 1 peer which is who I am comfortable with. However, new people I might not be so confidnet in asking for their help.


  • Asking coaches for help: I am confident in asking for help. I frequently meet up with DavID a few times a week to help my understanding of concepts which has been a big help. However, I have not talked to any other facilitators yet.


  • Improving my process with relfection: Reflection is something I frequently do to make sure I fully understand the content I am digesting so I am confident in my ability to reflect.
