3 Things I learnt about myself

I learnt how much I want to become a software developer, some goals and some weaknesses about myself. What made me realise how much I want to become a software developer is when I completed more javascript exercises. Doing these exercises, talking to people about them and working together to solve issues made me realize how much I wanted this than I had known before. Throughout doing them, I found out my goal is to find a company/group of people where I can work in a team environment and create/solve things all day. This became a goal because I found out how fun it is and would love for it to be my everyday life. However, one weakness I realized was that I compare myself to others and make myself feel worse when I do not fully understand things or can write the code that I envision. This is important as it is something that will be a daily part of my life in the bootcamp and need to work on.


What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Self-awareness is an important role to learning as knowing your own strengths and weaknesses are essential to moving forward towards a goal. If I don't know my own strengths and weaknesses it can take a lot longer to learn and interpret things. I struggle a lot with reading condensed areas of writing and have to find other ways to digest the content. Knowing this and acting on it is vital towards my success on learning anything. Values & empathy holds a role in learning as it helps taking the right deicisions in situations in learning. It is even more important in group learning as I will be working with people and having good and strong values will improve my relationship and connection with my peers & teachers in my learning experience.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

I think what surprised me the most about the core learning was what I learnt about myself. The core learning was something I never really gave much thought outside of this course and was never something I had previously explorered on my own or ever had to. Having to explore these areas and learning about myself was surprising.


What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

I think the most challenging aspect of the core learning was thinking like a programmer, identifying my values and strengths. It is something I have never thought about before and having to think about it was a challenge as I had to dig deep to get some answers. And thinking like a programmer is a way of thinking that is completely foreign to me as prior I graduated with a Bachelors of Business so they way of thinking is drastically different.


Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I think the use of focusing on core learning helped me become more confident in myself and understand how I behave and think. Knowing yourself inside and out is an advantage when taking on a new intense learning experience as I know I will be facing new difficulties and stress. Knowing how to deal with them, how I respond, what I need and being self aware will help me throughout these potential future struggles.


Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead?

It does not feel like a waste of time as it wsa a learning experience about myself and is useful information I can use for myself in future scenarios. The course taught me essential knowledge about coding but even more valuable information about myself which I would have not discovered anywhere else. I could have watched tutorials on Youtube or gone to another course and would not have discovered these new areas about myself.
