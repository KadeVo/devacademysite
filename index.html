What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding?

  • Margin: Margins are the transparent spaces around an element, outside of its border. They create space between the element and its neighboring elements. Margins do not have a background color or content and are used for visual separation or to control the positioning of elements. Margin values can be set in CSS to specify the amount of space desired around an element.
    Example Margin
  • Border: The border is a visible line or area that surrounds an element, enclosing its content and padding. It is located immediately outside the margin. Borders can be customized in terms of thickness, style, and color using CSS properties. They provide a visual distinction between the element and its surroundings.
    Example Border
  • Padding: Padding refers to the space between the content of an element and its border. It is the area inside the border and outside the content. Padding is used to create space between the content and the border, allowing for breathing room and separation. Padding values can also be defined using CSS, allowing you to specify the amount of space desired around the content.
    Example Padding
